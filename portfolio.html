"Chinsney"

"Chinsney"

When I first arrived in San Francisco, when I heard the words Chinatown, it always gave me a little "intimacy". After all, there was no such place when I was an undergraduate in Maryland. But it wasn't until I visited Chinatown once that my attitude towards this place changed. In my opinion, China in San Francisco is very absurd and ridiculous. There are many elements that do not appear in China. The themes of the so-called "Chinese characteristics" are often over-symbolicized. I did not see the scenes of affection. I only saw strange streets full of lanterns and some things that did not belong to me. Hometown antique buildings.

The excessive symbolization of Chinese elements in Chinatown can also contribute to the commercialization of Chinese culture, where it is used to attract tourists and sell products rather than being valued for its inherent cultural significance. This can lead to a disconnect between the symbols being used and the actual cultural practices and traditions they represent. One reason for this phenomenon is the historical and cultural differences between the East and West. For centuries, China has been seen as a distant, exotic land with a rich cultural heritage that has fascinated Westerners. As a result, Westerners may be drawn to simplified and superficial depictions of Chinese culture that reinforce preconceived notions or romanticized fantasies.

Therefore, this project hopes to approach the design and marketing of Chinatown in a way that is sensitive and respectful of the living experience and cultural traditions of the Chinese community. Create authentic representations of Chinese culture, celebrate the diversity of the community, and avoid simplistic and stereotyped depictions of Chinese culture.

Trash Can

Trash Can

The purpose of garbage classification is to increase the resource value and economic value of garbage, reduce the amount of garbage processed and the use of processing equipment, reduce processing costs, and reduce the consumption of land resources. It has social, economic, and ecological benefits.

According to data from the Shanghai Urban Management Bureau, in just four weeks since the introduction of the new policy, compared with the previous month, the daily recyclable waste increased by 10%, the kitchen waste increased by 15%, and the dry waste decreased by 11.7%. Based on Shanghai It generates 26,000 tons of garbage every day, which is a huge improvement.

The economic development model of recycling of renewable resources and continuous recycling of materials is becoming a global trend. The strategy of sustainable development has been unanimously agreed by everyone. Sustainable development is the development that meets the needs of contemporary human beings without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their needs. We pay attention to the quantity of economic growth and the quality of economic growth at the same time. The main sign is that resources can be used forever and a good ecological environment is maintained.

HangZhou City Sight

HangZhou City Sight

In the research on the impact of tourism development on tourist destinations, especially on the cultural heritage sites, commercialization issues have attracted the attention of many people, and their attitudes towards commercialization are often mixed. In fact, tourism and business have always been inseparable. The modern tourism economy is a tourism activity based on the market economy, with the production and exchange of tourism products as its main characteristics. And commerce, in essence, is an economic activity with profit-making purposes and commodity exchange as its characteristic.

With the change of dynasties in ancient China, the city of Hangzhou has undergone tremendous changes. It inherited 12 different names. Comparing the two ancient pictures above, we can see that in the past few hundred years, the size of the West Lake, the scale of the city, and even the city walls have undergone tremendous changes. But in any case, with the progress of civilization, we can see less wild landscape and more urban development and cultural view.

There are many dynasties in China for thousands of years. Whenever there is a change of dynasty, most of the new dynasties will either plunder or burn the cultural relics left by the previous dynasties.

My map started with the Song Dynasty, and the capital of the Song Dynasty was in Hangzhou, so Hangzhou has been developed vigorously and left many monuments. But with the changes in history, the later dynasties did not pay so much attention to the city, so many monuments were destroyed. But today, Hangzhou is a city with a very developed tourism industry, so many historic sites have been rebuilt.

Compressor

Compressor

To develop a compact indoor recycling bin specifically designed for cans, aiming to efficiently store a large number of cans in a limited space. The goal is to encourage people to recycle cans by making the process convenient and space-saving.

The Canpressor is designed to fit seamlessly into small rooms, with dimensions of 6 feet by 23.2 inches by 27 inches. Despite its narrow body, it can store up to 480 compressed cans (2 layers of 8 cans stacked 30 cans high) in the kitchen, living room, or any other space where the user enjoys a refreshing drink.

Halo

This project is an application design to help people who lost their jobs due to the epidemic get income.

Through designed an app to eliminate people’s stereotypes and bias for unemployed people in Baltimore. At the same time help them to integrate into society better by giving education and helping them find a job

Brainstorming

Visual Design

Logo Design

Final Product

The "Langtili Children's Formula Nutritional Milk Powder" is a specially designed milk powder for children aged 3 to 14 years. This product was a major design project I led at the design studio.

The design emphasizes modernity and strength, aligning with the brand's vision of promoting smart, strong, and healthy growth in children. The packaging design showcases these values through its creative visuals and branding elements, creating a strong association between the brand and its benefits for child development. The design also aims to lower communication costs effectively and establish a distinctive brand identity in the market.

Miderr Cat Snack

Miderr Cat Snack

Design Layout





Final Product

The "Miderr Pet Snacks" represent a cornerstone in pet care, specifically catering to the needs of young pet owners in China, predominantly post-2000s generations. These pet owners are the driving force behind the pet snack market, and understanding their preferences was crucial to our design approach.

In response to this demographic, we conducted a thorough market analysis, including 100 targeted samplings, to determine the exact consumer needs and desires. This research informed every aspect of our design, ensuring that the packaging not only resonated with the consumers' tastes but also maintained practical considerations such as shelf space efficiency.

We optimized the package size to avoid the perception of emptiness—a common concern with oversized packaging—while ensuring the product stood out on the shelves. The design strategy was multi-faceted, aiming to maintain the package's standing posture for visibility and to captivate consumers amidst a sea of competitors. This careful balance of form and function, coupled with a deep understanding of our core consumer base, resulted in a packaging that is not only aesthetically pleasing but also fulfills the practical requirements of retail display and consumer appeal.
